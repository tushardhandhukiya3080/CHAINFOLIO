NFTs, DeFi & DAOs

With blockchain, crypto, and smart contracts in hand, you can now understand the big applications people actually build. These three are the pillars of today's Web3 ecosystem.

🎨 NFTs — Non-Fungible Tokens

"Fungible" means interchangeable: one dollar equals any other dollar. A non-fungible token is unique — each one is one-of-a-kind and provably owned by a single wallet. NFTs are how you put ownership of a specific digital item on the blockchain.

Fungible (e.g. ETH) — every unit is identical and swappable.
Non-fungible (NFT) — each token has a unique ID; no two are the same.

Beyond art, NFTs can represent event tickets, in-game items, memberships, domain names, and certificates — anything where uniqueness and ownership matter.

Common myth: Owning an NFT usually means owning a verifiable record of ownership on-chain — not necessarily the copyright to the artwork. Always check what rights actually come with it.

🏦 DeFi — Decentralized Finance

DeFi rebuilds financial services — lending, borrowing, trading, saving — using smart contracts instead of banks. It's open to anyone with a wallet, runs 24/7, and is transparent by default.

💱

DEX

Decentralized exchanges (e.g. Uniswap) let you swap tokens directly from your wallet — no broker.

📈

Lending

Deposit assets to earn interest, or borrow against collateral — all enforced by code (e.g. Aave).

🪙

Stablecoins

Tokens pegged to a stable value like the US dollar (e.g. USDC) to avoid crypto's volatility.

Reality check: DeFi removes middlemen but not risk — smart-contract bugs, scams, and volatility are real. "Do your own research" is the culture for a reason.

🏛️ DAOs — Decentralized Autonomous Organizations

A DAO is an organization run by its community through code and voting, rather than by a CEO and a boardroom. Rules live in smart contracts; members hold governance tokens that let them propose and vote on decisions — like how to spend a shared treasury.

  • Transparent — proposals, votes, and funds are all on-chain for anyone to see.
  • Global — members can participate from anywhere.
  • Code-enforced — approved decisions can execute automatically.

🎓 You've completed the foundations!

You now understand the full stack of Web3: blockchains store tamper-evident data, cryptocurrencies and wallets move and prove ownership of value, smart contracts add programmable logic, and NFTs, DeFi, and DAOs are the applications built on top.
